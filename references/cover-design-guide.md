# Cover Design Guide — Genre-Specific Rules

## Contemporary / Office Romance

### Visual Style
- **Primary**: Flat modern illustration, warm saturated colors, minimal facial detail
- **Alternative**: Moody photorealistic for darker/spicier sub-genres
- Characters fill 60%+ of the frame
- Maximum 3 visual elements (2 characters + 1 setting anchor)

### Color Palettes by Sub-Genre
| Sub-Genre | Primary | Secondary | Accent |
|-----------|---------|-----------|--------|
| Rom-com office | Coral, peach | Teal, cream | Gold, warm yellow |
| Boss romance | Navy, charcoal | White, silver | Metallic gold |
| Dark/spicy office | Black, deep burgundy | Charcoal | Hot pink, red |
| Enemies-to-lovers | Electric teal | Deep navy | Coral, orange |
| Small town romance | Sage green, butter yellow | Warm cream | Dusty rose |

### Emotional Body Language (Sexual Tension)
- **Forbidden**: She looks up with guarded defiance, he watches with intensity he's hiding
- **Rivals**: Arms crossed, facing each other, electric proximity, neither backing down
- **Fake dating**: Bodies too close for pretending, hand at small of back, chin tilted up
- **Secret**: Stolen glance, hands almost touching, dangerous intimacy
- **Second chance**: Electric distance, one looking away while the other watches
- **Slow burn**: Drawn together across a table, losing composure

### Office-Specific Props
Loosened ties, fitted blazers, coffee cups, red pens, laptops, manuscripts, press releases, cameras, garment racks, event planning boards, financial reports, city skylines through glass

### Typography
- **Title**: Bold brush script OR heavy sans-serif. Large. High contrast against background.
- **Author name**: Clean sans-serif capitals. White or light color. Bottom center.
- **Maximum 2 fonts** (title + author)
- Title must be readable at 200x300px

## GPT-4o Prompt Template

```
Create a contemporary romance book cover illustration.

Style: Modern flat illustration with warm saturated colors, similar to bestselling contemporary romance covers like "The Hating Game" or "Beach Read." Minimal facial detail on characters — readers should be able to project themselves. Professional book cover quality, not stock art.

Scene: {SCENE — specific setting with 1-2 anchor details that establish location}

Characters: {CHARACTER_1 — ethnicity, hair, outfit, distinguishing feature, pose}. {CHARACTER_2 — ethnicity, build, outfit, distinguishing feature, pose}.

Emotion: {TENSION_TYPE — specific body language description conveying sexual tension. Be explicit about eye contact, physical proximity, hand placement, body angles.}

Color palette: {COLOR_1}, {COLOR_2}, {ACCENT}. Warm overall tone with {mood} lighting.

Composition: Characters fill the upper two-thirds of the frame. {SETTING_ANCHOR} visible behind them. Clean dark/gradient space at the very bottom for author name. Title text positioned at {POSITION — center, upper third, overlapping mid-section}.

Title text "{BOOK_TITLE}" in bold {FONT_STYLE — brush script / heavy sans-serif} font, {TITLE_COLOR}, large and prominently placed. The title must be clearly legible even when the cover is displayed at thumbnail size (200x300 pixels).

Author name "{AUTHOR_NAME}" in clean white sans-serif capital letters at the bottom center of the cover.

Technical: Aspect ratio exactly 10:16 (portrait orientation, book cover proportions). This cover will be displayed on Amazon's Kindle Store alongside professional traditionally-published covers — it must look equally polished and visually striking, especially as a small thumbnail in search results. Bold color blocking, high contrast, strong silhouette.
```

## Thumbnail Strength Checklist

At 200x300px:
- [ ] Title instantly readable (no squinting)
- [ ] Color palette creates a strong visual "shape"
- [ ] Characters clearly identifiable as people
- [ ] Genre immediately recognizable (romance, not thriller)
- [ ] Would not look out of place next to "The Hating Game" or "Beach Read" thumbnails

## Common AI Cover Mistakes to Avoid

- Extra fingers or limbs
- Warped or misspelled text
- Uncanny valley faces (use "minimal facial detail" in prompt)
- Over-busy compositions that become noise at small size
- Generic stock-photo aesthetic
- Text placed over busy/detailed backgrounds (unreadable)
- Inconsistent lighting between characters and background
