# Scene-Sequel Structure

Every chapter should contain at least one complete Scene-Sequel cycle.

## SCENE (Action)

The active, forward-moving unit of story.

### Components:
1. **Goal** — What the POV character wants in this scene (specific, concrete)
2. **Conflict** — What prevents them from getting it (obstacles, opposition)
3. **Disaster** — The outcome, which must be WORSE than expected
   - Not just failure — a new complication
   - The disaster creates a new problem the character didn't have before

### Rules:
- Enter late: skip the setup, start with the goal already in motion
- Every scene needs conflict — even "quiet" scenes have tension
- The disaster is what hooks the reader into the sequel
- Physical scenes: shorter paragraphs, shorter sentences, active verbs

## SEQUEL (Reaction)

The reactive, emotional unit that processes the disaster.

### Components:
1. **Reaction** — Emotional response to the disaster (visceral, then processed)
2. **Dilemma** — The character considers their options (all bad)
3. **Decision** — They choose a course of action, which becomes the next scene's GOAL

### Rules:
- Sequels are where character is revealed
- The reaction must feel authentic — don't skip to logic too fast
- The dilemma should present genuinely difficult choices
- The decision drives the story forward

## Pacing with Scene-Sequel

- **Fast pacing**: Short sequels, quick decisions, back into action
  (thrillers, action sequences, climax chapters)
- **Slow pacing**: Long sequels, deep reflection, emotional processing
  (romance, literary, character-study chapters)
- **Varying pace**: Alternate long and short sequels throughout the novel

## Common Mistakes

1. **Missing sequel**: Scene → Scene → Scene = exhausting, no emotional depth
2. **Missing scene**: Sequel → Sequel → Sequel = navel-gazing, no plot movement
3. **Easy disaster**: Character fails but nothing gets worse = no tension
4. **Obvious decision**: Only one logical choice = no dilemma
5. **Skipped reaction**: Character goes straight to logic = feels robotic
