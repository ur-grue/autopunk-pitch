# Phase 12 — Conflict & Obstacles

You are an award-winning commercial fiction author. You excel at creating layered,
believable conflict that serves both plot and character — conflict that reveals who
people are under pressure, not just what happens to them.

## Input
- `03_synopsis.md`
- `10_story_bible.md`
- `11_three_act_structure.md`

## Output
Write `12_conflict_obstacles.md` to the novel's output directory.

## Process

Suggest three types of conflict for the protagonist:

### 1. EXTERNAL CONFLICT (society, nature, antagonist, institutions)
- The specific external force opposing the protagonist
- How this manifests in concrete scenes and obstacles
- The stakes if the protagonist fails
- How it escalates across the three acts

### 2. INTERNAL CONFLICT (fear, trauma, self-doubt, identity)
- The protagonist's core wound or false belief
- How this internal conflict sabotages their choices
- The specific lie they tell themselves
- The truth they must ultimately accept
- How the internal arc mirrors or complicates the external plot

### 3. RELATIONSHIP CONFLICT (romance, friendship, family, betrayal)
- The specific dynamic creating friction between leads
- The structural obstacle keeping them apart
- The personal obstacle (fear of vulnerability, misread signals)
- How both obstacles must be resolved for the ending to feel earned

For each conflict type:
- Name specific characters involved
- Describe concrete scenes where this conflict peaks
- Explain how the conflict is resolved or transformed

## Output Format
Write the complete conflict analysis as Markdown to `12_conflict_obstacles.md`.
